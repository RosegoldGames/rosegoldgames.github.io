The Otherworld

The Otherworld


A skybound world of floating lands, ancient magic, and cultures that never meant to cross paths…

Introduction

The Otherworld takes place in a fantasy medieval setting, in a world that is made up of large chunks of land floating in the sky. There are different regions of land, each looking very unique and out-of-place from the other. It is as if the Otherworld wasn’t supposed to exist naturally. Each land has its own types of stone, foliage, trees, and even sky.

Different types of people occupy each region. They live in the borders of their lands, each race of people having their own unique culture, and living a completely different lifestyle. Some have plentiful resources and are advancing in technology. Others prefer to be one with their nature, living off their land.

In a world of collided chaos, many races decide to stick to themselves in their own regions. Some cultures are diplomatic, while others take a more aggressive stance to outsiders.

World Regions

The Mainlands

The Mainlands is a vast grassy meadow, home to the Human race. It holds cities made of brick and copper, with some houses even utilizing the recent discovery of electrical lighting. Main resources include copper and silver, which are used for currency and everyday life. Humans are very diplomatic and try to maintain peace and connections, while advancing quickly in technology and discovery.

The Deepwoods

The Deepwoods is a coniferous redwood taiga, home to the Oni race. They are much like humans, but are larger and stronger. They also have pointed ears and horns growing from their heads. They live together in campsites; common homes are teepees, with campfires to stay warm from the cold nights. The Deepwoods is full of valuable resources like iron and plentiful yet dangerous animals. The Oni do not welcome outsiders warmly, especially those who practice magic. They keep the peace with humans, however.

Faiechantia

Faiechantia is a magical and ancient forest, home to the Fairie race. The Fairies are naturally in tune with magic and hold many tales and secrets of the world. They live within the ancient trees themselves, with boardwalks connecting their treetop houses. Valuable resources include Opal, a sturdy gemstone with magical powers used for crafting weapons and tools. Fairies mostly keep to themselves to protect their ancient ways. They only open up to those who are in tune with magic themselves and respect their ways.

Rosquartzia

Rosquartzia is another magical land, made of rose-quartz crystal fields. It is home to the Roseian race, who are natural-born users of healing magic. They live in small villages and are known to be calm, friendly, protective, and sensitive to emotions. They are direct allies with the Fairies and rely on the Fairies’ strength and fighting knowledge to survive an ongoing conflict with the Oni. In return, the Roseians heal Fairies from wounds and any kind of ailments. Common resources are Rosegold for coins, tools, and daily life, and Rose-Quartz for building materials.

Glaceia

Glaceia is a freezing land full of snow and ice, home to the Iceian race. The Iceians look as if they were sculpted from blue ice, their skin glossy and frozen. They are stubborn, strong, and logical, and interactions can be cold. However, they have a warm side. They strongly focus on caring for family and are very loyal to friends. Due to their extreme environment, Iceians are most comfortable being frozen and despise the heat. They rarely venture beyond Glaceia’s boundaries due to the climate of the other regions. On the rare occasions they do, they utilize Sapphire, a common valuable resource, to craft armor. This protects them from heat and outside elements. Iceians live in close settlements, varying from igloos to buildings sculpted from dense ice.

Elaria

Elaria is a temperate and lush swampy jungle, full of vines and poisonous plants. It is home to the Elarian race, green sentient plant people with vines for hair. They live in a sheltered tribal setting, utilizing natural poisons to protect their lands from outsiders with ill intentions. They live in primal tribes, crafting weapons and tools made of Jade.

Emberia

Emberia is a glowing-hot lava-filled world, home to the Emberian race. They are the polar opposite of Iceians, with skin made of ash and hair glowing with fire. Emberians are most comfortable in the sweltering heat, and the hotter it is, the more energy they have. Emberians are the second most advanced race besides humans. They live in compact cities under a militant empire, with fire-immune buildings made of compacted ash and other igneous rocks. Topaz is a very common resource in this region, and the Emberians utilize it for coins, weapons, and tools. Topaz emits heat naturally, so armor is made of it to keep Emberians burning, even in cold environments. Emberians have an ongoing conflict with Iceians due to their elemental nature and conflicting cultures.

Gameplay

The Main Loop

Explore – Battle – Gather – Upgrade – Progress

Explore

Traverse the floating island terrain with the risk of falling into the void below. Design obstacles like Emberian lava lakes, Iceian ice that slides the player, and Elarian vines and poisonous fog.

Battle

Regions have varying levels of difficulty and combat mechanics. Humans have developed and advanced weaponry, Oni have natural brute strength, Fairies have skilled tree scouts and magical spells, Roseians have magical traps to protect themselves, Iceians and Emberians have elemental protection like freezing temperatures and lava hazards, and Elarians use poison for defense.

Gather

The player will gather resources of varying rarity through exploration and combat.

Upgrade

The player will use these resources to craft gear tailored to their needs and their own race. They can trade, buy, or make better gear and weapons; use region-specific resources like Opal to channel magical abilities, Jade to make poisonous weapons, Topaz for heat and armor if the player is Emberian and for burning weapons, Sapphire for cold and armor if an Iceian player and for weapons that freeze and stun enemies. Roseians can provide healing antidotes.

Progress

Gathering resources, upgrading gear, and mastering difficult terrain obstacles and combat mechanics will lead to unlocking regions of varying difficulty dependent on the player’s race. The player can still physically traverse these “locked” regions, but death will be almost certain if the player is not prepared, and the cost is that they will lose their gathered items and their progress.
